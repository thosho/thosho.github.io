Our Story

Where India's Soul
Meets Modern Mastery

Saffron Table was born from a singular conviction: that the complexity, layering, and emotional depth of Indian cuisine deserves a world-class stage. Nestled in the heart of Banjara Hills, our restaurant reimagines the subcontinent's most beloved culinary traditions through a modern lens — treating spices not as flavours but as narratives.

Every element — from our hand-thrown terracotta serving pieces to our handwritten seasonal menus — is a deliberate act of reverence for the cuisines of India's 29 states, interpreted with the precision of a Michelin-trained kitchen.

A

Chef Arjun Sharma

Executive Chef & Co-Founder. Formerly of The Taj Rambagh Palace Jaipur and Benares, London. Trained under Atul Kochhar. Arjun's philosophy centres on hyperlocal sourcing, classical Awadhi and Chettinad techniques, and the elegant restraint of less-is-more plating.

Tender free-range chicken breast, 48-hour marinated in cultured cream, cardamom-rose water brine and Kashmiri saffron. Kissed by a charcoal tandoor, served with micro herb chutney and pickled green apple.

Jhinga Ajwain

₹1,500

Bay of Bengal tiger prawns, dry-rubbed with carom seeds, raw mango powder and Tellicherry pepper, flash-grilled and finished with a clarified ghee and fresh dill compound butter. Served with coastal coconut-tomato relish.

Signature

Pani Puri Sphere

₹800

A modern interpretation of India's most beloved street snack. A single, perfectly crisp semolina sphere presented tableside — the waiter pours iced tamarind-jaljeera water directly at the moment of service. A burst of nostalgia, reimagined.

Avant-Garde

Dal Chawal Arancini

₹900

An East-meets-West ode to the great dal chawal. Crispy risotto-style rice balls bound with slow-cooked toor dal, filled with smoked mozzarella and deep-fried golden. Accompanied by a silky tomato-achaar coulis.

Fusion

Dal Makhani Truffle

₹1,800

Our legendary 72-hour slow-cooked black lentil and kidney bean preparation, enriched with Amul cultured butter, Kashmiri cream and elevated with a finishing drizzle of black truffle oil. Served with house-made laccha paratha.

Chef's Pride

Raan-e-Awadh

₹3,200

A whole slow-roasted leg of Rajasthani lamb, marinated for 36 hours in a regal blend of over 40 spices, braised in a sealed degchi and finished over smouldering coals. The dish that defines dum cooking. Serves two.

Signature · Serves 2

Malabar Prawn Moilee

₹2,800

A refined coastal classic from the Malabar shores of Kerala. Jumbo king prawns simmered in a delicate first-press coconut milk broth, seasoned with turmeric and fresh green chillies. Served with fragrant Kerala red rice and crispy appam.

Coastal

Paneer Shahi Korma

₹1,600

House-made organic milk paneer nestled in a velvety Mughal-era korma of blanched almond paste, saffron-milk reduction, caramelised onions and aromatic khoya. Finished with edible silver leaf and a rose petal garnish.

Vegetarian

A seamless marriage of French technique and Bengali heritage. A cushion of hand-squeezed chhena set in cardamom-saffron rabdi cream, topped with a mirror-crack caramel brûlée crust. Served with pistachio praline crumble.
